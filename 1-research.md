# Aussie Broadband Support Policy Research

## Purpose

Summarise the public policies and service rules most relevant to an AI-assisted Gmail-to-ticket workflow.

## 1. Business Context

Aussie Broadband provides residential, business, enterprise, government, and wholesale telecommunications services.

Relevant support areas include internet, mobile, phone/VoIP, hardware, installation, relocation, billing, cancellation, complaints, hardship, privacy, and outages.

Support channels include phone, live chat, MyAussie, Help Centre, outage tools, and limited accessibility email support.

## 2. Customer Service Commitments

Aussie Broadband states that it aims to:

- Be open, honest, and fair
- Use plain-English information
- Resolve disputes quickly and fairly
- Support vulnerable customers
- Provide an appropriate remedy when required

Ticket replies should be clear, practical, respectful, and transparent.

## 3. Billing and Payments

- Invoices are generally issued monthly.
- Payment is due within **10 business days**.
- A declined bank or direct-debit payment may incur a **$5.50 fee**.
- A declined credit-card payment may incur a **single $10 late fee for that month**.
- Customers expecting difficulty should contact support before the debit date.
- Unresolved non-payment may lead to restriction and later termination.

Restricted services may include internet limited to approximately **1:1 Mbps**, VoIP or mobile limited to emergency use, and blocked Fetch purchases.

The AI must not waive fees, confirm payment outcomes, or promise restoration.

## 4. Financial Hardship

Hardship assistance is free and may include:

- Payment plans
- Spend controls
- Temporary plan downgrades
- Service restrictions
- Individual payment arrangements
- Possible late-fee waivers

Possible hardship circumstances include illness, unemployment, reduced income, family or domestic violence, bereavement, natural disaster, or unexpected financial difficulty.

Hardship requests require sensitive handling and human review.

## 5. Complaints

A complaint includes dissatisfaction with products, services, billing, fault handling, staff interactions, or previous support outcomes.

Published timeframes include:

- Acknowledgement within **2 working days**
- Investigation contact within **10 working days** for unresolved matters
- Agreed resolution implemented within **5 working days**, unless an exception applies
- Urgent resolution attempted within **2 working days**

Urgent indicators include hardship, disconnection without due process, priority-assistance impact, family or domestic violence, and serious safety or health risk.

Unresolved matters may be escalated to a Complaints Manager and then the Telecommunications Industry Ombudsman.

The AI must not promise complaint success or a final outcome.

## 6. Outages and Service Disruption

Useful investigation details include:

- Service address or account reference
- Service type
- Start time and duration
- Devices affected
- Wi-Fi, Ethernet, or full-service impact
- Troubleshooting already attempted
- Any outage notification received

For significant outages, updates may occur at least every **6 hours during the first 24 hours**, then at least once per following 24-hour period.

Urgent outage indicators include personal safety risk, serious health risk, priority-assistance impact, or business-critical service loss.

The AI must not promise interim services or restoration times.

## 7. Outage Compensation

Published compensation generally requires:

- An unplanned outage or fault
- Disruption lasting at least **2 days from first contact**
- A reasonable opportunity for Aussie Broadband to restore service
- Assessment after restoration

Compensation is generally based on the affected Aussie Broadband service only. Third-party costs are generally not reimbursed.

The AI must not confirm eligibility or promise compensation, refunds, or credits.

## 8. Cancellation and Closure

- Residential broadband is generally month-to-month.
- Some business services may have contract terms.
- Closure requires the **primary account holder**.
- Phone or LiveChat verification and **two-factor authentication** are required.
- Pro-rata closure may be requested.

The AI may route a cancellation request but must not confirm completion.

## 9. Relocation and Plan Changes

- Relocation generally has no dedicated fee.
- Extra charges may apply for new developments, first-time connections, or hardware.
- Plan changes through MyAussie generally start at the next billing cycle.
- Immediate or mid-cycle changes may require customer-service assistance.

The AI must not promise activation timing, final charges, or price adjustments.

## 10. Installation and Connection

Typical public estimates include:

- Fully installed service connection: **1–5 days**
- nbn® technician appointment: approximately **1–4 weeks**
- OptiComm appointment: approximately **1–2 weeks**

Possible extra costs include new-development fees, first-time connection fees, incorrect callout fees, hardware, shipping, or business setup charges.

These are estimates, not guaranteed deadlines.

## 11. Hardware, Warranty, and Returns

Published warranty periods include:

- NetComm devices: **24 months**
- Selected eero devices: **36 months**
- Other eero devices: **24 months**
- VoIP adaptors: **12 months**

Other rules:

- Faulty replacement hardware may need to be returned.
- Failure to return it within **30 business days** may result in a charge.
- Non-faulty hardware generally must be returned within **10 business days of purchase**.
- Refund or credit is assessed after receipt and testing.
- Exchanges are generally not offered for non-faulty returns.

The AI must not approve a refund, replacement, or warranty claim.

## 12. Privacy and Account Authority

Privacy, identity, or unauthorised-access concerns require human escalation.

The AI must not:

- Reveal account details before verification
- Accept passwords, PINs, passkeys, or SMS codes
- Confirm identity from email alone
- Change account authority
- Disclose information to an unverified third party

Advocates require the primary account holder's presence and consent. Authorised contacts have limited permissions and generally cannot close, relocate, or add services or contacts.

## 13. Vulnerability, Accessibility, and Bereavement

Aussie Broadband provides support for family and domestic violence, hardship, accessibility needs, National Relay Service users, and bereavement.

Family and domestic violence cases require confidentiality, empathy, and careful handling.

Bereavement requests may require account details and proof that the requester is an authorised representative, executor, administrator, solicitor, or next of kin.

These cases should be escalated to a trained human agent.

## 14. Workflow Implications

The AI may:

- Explain published policies and general next steps
- Request the minimum necessary information
- Classify priority and sentiment
- Recommend the correct support pathway
- Draft a cautious reply for human review

The AI must not:

- Approve refunds, credits, waivers, or compensation
- Confirm cancellation, relocation, or account changes
- Guarantee installation, restoration, or complaint timelines
- Make legal, fraud, or privacy-breach findings
- Send replies or take account action without human review
